New Ideas for Cycling and Pedestrian Projects

Event ▷ Onward
1,000 miles of bike lanes, 500 Citi Bike stations, and a decade of street reclamations have transformed the ways New Yorkers move. Yet other cities around the world are setting new standards with bike superhighways and pedestrianized city centers. Is it time for New York City to boldly build new pedestrian and cycling infrastructure? [More]
06.15.2016
New Ideas for Urban Freight
Event ▷ Onward
On June 15, the Urban Design Forum invited Jill Morgenweck, Director of Regional Operations at Shyp; Makoto Okazaki, Partner and Principal Architect at Michael Sorkin Studio; Paul Salama, Zoning + GIS Lead at Envelope; Juliette Spertus, Co-founder of ClosedLoops; and moderator Greg Lindsay to debate the future of urban freight.[More]
05.25.2016
New Ideas for Street Design
Event ▷ Onward
On May 25, the Urban Design Forum invited Kate Ascher, Partner at Buro Happold; Margaret Newman, Associate Principal at Arup; Paolo Santi, Research Scientist at MIT Senseable City Lab; and Catherine Seavitt, Principal of Catherine Seavitt Studio, to participate in our second roundtable on the future of transportation in New York City.
